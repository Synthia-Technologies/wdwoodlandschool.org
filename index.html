------ Our Mission & About Us ------

The West Denmark Woodland School is a state-licensed, nature-based early childhood center focusing on nature play and offering full-day programming for children ages 2.5 to 5 years. The Woodland School is founded on principles and practices of the Danish Forest School model (environmental/outdoor education) and the Italian Reggio Emilia model (inquiry and expression documented through the arts and photography).


------ Children are Instinctively Drawn to Nature ------

We are guided by the idea that children learn with enthusiasm and a sense of wonder when they spend ample time in nature. Play, imagination, and choice are recognized as vital to children's learning and development. While fostering whole-child development and nature connections, our Woodland School prepares young children to succeed in school and in life.



Some Features of Our Program:

- balancing logs - nature huts and loose parts building areas
- musical/sound exploration area - a natural stream for exploration
- documentation display - book nooks
- eating and napping areas - gathering areas (indoors, in our Nature Playscape, and on the Woodland Trail)





------ All Learners are Unique and Valuable ------

The curriculum assumes all learners can explore and make discoveries in nature and can develop positive relationships with each other and their environment. hildren are offered the opportunity to take supported risks appropriate to the environment and to themselves as learners. The curriculum emphasizes basic forest ecology and natural sciences, motor awareness, use of inquiry tools for investigations and the arts to express learnings, and healthy living as well as the beginnings of literacy awareness and mathematical reasoning.


------ Our Basic Curriculum Goals ------

  • To develop increased knowledge of biodiversity in nature and to distinguish different species or types,
  • To demonstrate increased emotional connectedness to and comfort in nature and to appreciate beauty in nature,
  • To demonstrate active care for the natural world, and
  • To represent nature observations and nature inquiry through various arts/documentation strategies.


Impact of a Nature Play/Forest School Program for the Child:

- increases confidence and improves view of self as learner - increases focus and attention skills
- increases creativity and appreciation for beauty in the natural world - increases communication, story-building, and representational skills
- improves socialization and self-regulation - encourages curiosity in the natural world
- improves balance, coordination, and motor skills - increases problem-solving skills
- learns how to take healthy risks And Much More





------ Beginnings ------

We are located on ancestral lands of the Anishinaabe peoples or the Chippewa First Nation. The Anishinaabe cared for Wisconsin's woodlands for thousands of years and regarded them as a gift from the Great Spirit. The woodlands provided Chippewa families with food and materials for their homes, tools, ornaments, toys, and transportation as well as artistic and spiritual inspiration. We are committed to acknowledging and honoring the history, culture, and current lives of the Anishinaabe peoples as we engage with and learn from nature.

------ FAQs ------

What is a Forest School? A Forest School is an international approach to early learning that originated in Denmark in the 1950s and takes place primarily outdoors, exposing learners to all they can learn about the nature and the natural world. With a renaissance of interest in outdoor learning in the United States about 20 years ago, the Forest School has grown to a popular, nation-wide approach in the early education. Forest Schools emphasize the importance of play, exploration, supported risk-taking, and a strong connection with the natural environment. In addition to these key principles, our Woodland School program emphasizes individual and group expression and documentation of learning through the arts.
What are the Daily Hours of Program Operation? The program operates M-F from 7:30 am to 5:30 pm, except for designated holidays or vacation days. These include: Memorial Day (Federal Holiday), West Denmark Fiddle School week in mid-June, West Denmark Family Camp, June 30-July 3, July 4th, Labor Day (Federal Holiday), Thanksgiving Thursday and Friday, and the week between Christmas and New Year's Day, December 24-January 1.
Is Subsidy Available? Families may qualify for Employer-Sponsored assistance or the Wisconsin Shares Program. For the Wisconsin Shares Program, you will need to pay your subsidy amount utilizing your “My WI Child Care” card via telephone or internet.
Are Lunch and Snacks Provided? No, please send a packed lunch with your child. We will provide 2 snacks (mid-morning and mid-afternoon).
Is There Drinking Water Available on Site? In our program, we offer a filtered, bottle-filling station as well as drinking fountain.
What are Some of the Things My Child Needs to Bring? The following clothing/supply requirements are for everyone attending a Woodland School session:

Winter: 2 winter hats, Fleece neck warmer or balaclava, 2 pairs of warm, waterproof mitts, 1 warm, waterproof snowsuit, 1 set of warm, waterproof boots (preferably with a removable liner), 3 base layers-thermal top/shirt and pants, middle layer, wool/fleece sweater, and 2 pairs of warm, wool socks.
Spring and Fall: See Summer list; plus a warmer sweater or jacket, long pants and long-sleeve top/shirt, gloves for cool weather, base layers, and extra boots (rain, hiking).
Summer: Rain gear (waterproof boots or wellies, waterproof over-pants, and waterproof jacket), hiking or running shoes, sun hat, lightweight clothing (but long pants and long-sleeve top/shirt), bug and tick spray, and sunscreen.
All Seasons: Small backpack (daypack), water bottle, sunglasses, change of clothes (especially socks!), and sleeping bag.

There is no need to go with expensive gear - check out the local thrift shops for good quality things at fraction of the cost!
Where is the West Denmark Woodland School Located? The West Denmark Woodland School is located at 2478 170th Street, Luck, Wisconsin 54853. For directions from your location, please see Google Maps or MapQuest.
What if my Child Needs to Go to the Bathroom While in the Woods? The Woodland School follows Leave No Trace (LNT) principles as best as possible when students need to use the washroom away from our main facilities. Children will use a foldable OXO Tot Potty with liners. Staff are prepared with hand washing kits (i.e., pump thermos with hot water and a soap dispenser and other material needed to do this successfully).
What about Extreme Weather Days? Whether we have rain, sun, wind, or snow, we can learn outdoors. When children are dressed appropriately, they are able to become deeply engaged in play and are not bothered by the "bad" weather. Weather creates an opportunity to learn about the ever-changing seasons and the impacts they have on our landscapes. Children learn to embrace and to find joy in every type of weather that western Wisconsin brings.

That said, we take precautions and stay indoors when weather conditions call for it. We follow the guidelines of the Wisconsin Child Care Weather Watch Chart, taking into account both the wind-chill factor and relative humidity as well as temperature and storm conditions.
